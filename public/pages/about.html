About

xaround exists because I genuinely believe campuses—universities and colleges—deserve better than temporary solutions. From my own experience, many campuses rely on volunteer-run Facebook groups or Telegram channels combined with Google Forms for anonymous posts, setups that often fall apart once administrators graduate or move on. These makeshift arrangements frequently erase valuable shared knowledge, interrupt conversations, and leave communities fragmented and disconnected.

I've often been asked, "Why do you care so much about campus communities?" My answer is simple and personal: campuses are unique, dynamic environments filled with talented and diverse individuals who are figuring out their lives, their studies, and their futures. Each student is on their own path, yet surrounded by immense opportunities to collaborate. Connecting these people matters deeply to me because when diverse minds meet, incredible things happen—like discovering future co-founders, collaborating on interdisciplinary projects, or simply forming friendships across different fields and campuses.

Life at university can be challenging. Even though campuses are filled with thousands of students, it's surprisingly easy to feel isolated or overlooked. Often, connections are limited to classmates or club members, leaving many students feeling disconnected from the broader community. I created xaround specifically to address these realities:

  • The loneliness of feeling alone despite being surrounded by so many people.
  • The need for a safe, supportive space to vent or seek comfort after difficult experiences like tough days or breakups.
  • The struggles of navigating personal issues, whether they involve family, finances, mental health, or any other unexpected life challenges.

xaround aims to provide exactly the kind of genuine space I wish had existed during my university years—a space built specifically for campuses, designed to endure beyond individual administrators, protecting privacy through thoughtful AI-driven moderation, and enabling structured, meaningful conversations without delays.

At its core, xaround is simply about fostering authentic interactions, openness, understanding, and meaningful relationships. It's not about marketing, sales, or convincing anyone—it's purely my heartfelt attempt to create a platform that can genuinely help students build connections and friendships that might otherwise never happen.
